## Analysis Summary

### Data Cleaning
- Removed 8 redundant imperial-unit columns
- Parsed datetime and extracted year, month, day, hour, season
- Capped outliers using IQR winsorizing on 9 key columns
- Mapped 211 countries to continents (including multilingual country names)
- Final dataset: 144,822 rows x 33 columns, 0 missing values

### Basic EDA
- Global average temperature: ~21°C
- Hottest countries: Middle East and Sub-Saharan Africa
- Coldest countries: Russia, Canada, Nordic nations
- Precipitation is highly right-skewed (most days have 0mm)
- Strong correlation between temperature and feels_like, UV index

### Anomaly Detection
- Isolation Forest detected 4,345 anomalies (3% of dataset)
- LOF confirmed anomaly pattern on 20,000-row sample
- Anomaly profile: higher wind (+49%), higher PM2.5 (+100%), lower pressure

### Forecasting Models

Three models were trained and evaluated on a 60-day test set:

- ARIMA(5,1,2): MAE 2.14, RMSE 2.45, R2 -3.13, MAPE 10.12%
- Linear Regression: MAE 0.30, RMSE 0.36, R2 0.91, MAPE 1.45%
- XGBoost: MAE 0.29, RMSE 0.36, R2 0.91, MAPE 1.39%

XGBoost achieved the best individual performance.
ARIMA underperformed on globally-aggregated data due to lack of autocorrelation structure.

### Ensemble Models


- Simple Average Ensemble: MAE 0.77, RMSE 0.91, R2 0.43, MAPE 3.64%
- Weighted Average Ensemble: MAE 0.32, RMSE 0.39, R2 0.90, MAPE 1.52%

The weighted ensemble assigned roughly 98% of its weight to Linear
Regression and XGBoost, neutralizing ARIMA's poor performance.

### Climate Analysis
- Slight warming trend detected over the 2-year period
- Africa and South America are the warmest continents
- Clear seasonal cycles visible in all continents
- Southern hemisphere seasons are inverted vs Northern hemisphere

### Environmental Impact
- Asia has the highest PM2.5 and PM10 pollution levels
- Wind speed has a strong negative correlation with PM2.5
- Humidity has a positive correlation with PM2.5 (traps particles)
- Majority of observations fall in the Good/Moderate AQI category

### Feature Importance
- feels_like_celsius is the dominant feature (direct temperature proxy)
- Humidity and wind_kph are the strongest independent predictors
- SHAP values confirm seasonal features (month, day_of_year) drive temperature
- All 4 methods (RF, XGBoost, Permutation, SHAP) agree on feature rankings

### Spatial Analysis
- Interactive choropleth maps for temperature and PM2.5 by country
- Folium heatmap shows clear temperature gradient from equator to poles
- K-Means clustering identified 5 distinct global weather clusters
- Strong negative correlation between latitude and temperature confirmed

---

## Key Findings

1. XGBoost with lag features outperforms classical ARIMA for global temperature forecasting
2. Anomalous weather events are characterized by high wind, low pressure, and doubled PM2.5
3. Asia has significantly worse air quality than other continents
4. Temperature is strongly driven by seasonal and humidity features
5. Global weather stations cluster into 5 distinct climate profiles
